# JohnWhitton.com Site Strategy

This document guides the refactor of johnwhitton.com so the site aligns with the latest portfolio work, current professional positioning, and long-term identity of John Whitton.

## Vision

johnwhitton.com is the professional home for John Whitton.

The site should position John as:

```text
Engineer. Architect. Leader.
```

working across:

* Trading Systems
* Financial Infrastructure
* DeFi Protocol Engineering
* Distributed Systems
* AI Engineering

The site should remain durable even after John finds a new role, starts a company, or changes his immediate professional focus.

## Positioning

johnwhitton.com is the home for:

* Professional identity
* Biography
* Career narrative
* Resume
* References
* Contact and introduction paths
* Links to portfolio, research, and GitHub

The site should explain who John is, what he has done, and why his background matters.

johnwhitton.com is not:

* A single job-search campaign page
* A research studio website
* A product website
* A portfolio slide deck
* A crypto-only profile
* An arbitrage-only profile

Instead, it is the stable professional identity layer that connects the person, the proof of work, and current research.

## Site Structure

The site should remain simple and stable.

Target structure:

```text
Home
|-- About
|-- Resume
|-- References
|-- GitHub
`-- External Links
    |-- Portfolio
    |-- Jincubator Research
    `-- LinkedIn / contact paths
```

Do not add Portfolio as a top-level nav item unless later review shows the homepage, About, Resume, and References links are insufficient.

Portfolio should be strongly featured through page CTAs where it supports the page purpose, especially on Home and Resume. Additional portfolio entry points can be reconsidered during final navigation review.

## Page Strategy

### Home

Purpose:

```text
Create the first impression.
```

The homepage should immediately establish:

* John Whitton
* Engineer. Architect. Leader.
* Five domains
* Portfolio preview
* Clear paths to About, Resume, Research, and Portfolio

The homepage should not be a long biography.

### About

Purpose:

```text
Explain John’s story.
```

The About page should be narrative, not resume-like.

It should answer:

* Who is John?
* How did his career evolve?
* Why does his background connect enterprise systems, blockchain, payments, trading, and AI?
* What kinds of systems does he build?
* What kind of collaborator and leader is he?

The About page should be durable and should not feel like a temporary job-search page.

### Resume

Purpose:

```text
Help recruiters and hiring teams evaluate role fit.
```

The Resume page should be structured, factual, and role-oriented.

It should include:

* Executive summary
* Resume variants
* Career snapshot
* Selected systems
* Professional experience
* Downloadable PDFs
* Links to portfolio and GitHub

The Resume page can change more frequently than the About page.

### References

Purpose:

```text
Provide social proof.
```

The References page should show what it is like to work with John.

It should include:

* Testimonials
* Team feedback
* Reference summaries
* Leadership themes
* Existing Harmony team material where useful

References should support claims about leadership, mentorship, technical judgment, and execution.

### Portfolio Links

Purpose:

```text
Move technical reviewers from identity to proof.
```

Portfolio links should appear prominently on:

* Home
* About
* Resume
* References

Preferred CTA language:

```text
View Portfolio
```

or:

```text
Open Portfolio
```

## Refactor Principles

* Keep the site simple.
* Preserve a durable professional identity.
* Use the portfolio as proof, not as the entire site.
* Keep About narrative and Resume factual.
* Make the five domains consistent across pages.
* Keep “Engineer. Architect. Leader.” visible and central.
* Make it easy for visitors to choose their path:

  * learn the story
  * review the resume
  * inspect the portfolio
  * read references
  * visit research
* Do not make johnwhitton.com feel like a temporary job campaign.
* Do not make johnwhitton.com feel like only a crypto website.
* Do not make johnwhitton.com feel like only a research site.
